Dropbox recently released a new product, Dropbox Paper beta. It is generally well-designed, but some design problems were revealed after a quick usability test.

Objective

Identify pain points in editing documents in Dropbox Paper beta.

Questions I wanted to answer

Can users easily editing the doc using the following features?

  • Headings, body (different font size)
  • Embedding video & musics
  • Comments + Stickers, Emojis
  • Uploading and placing pictures
  • @someone,
  • Strikethrough
  • Bulletin list, task list, number list

Here is the sample link document I asked my participants to recreate on their laptop.

Tasks

I provided the sample document for my participants, including the pictures, music and video in Google Chrome web browser. I asked my participants to do 3 following things:

  1. Create a new doc
  2. Edit a new doc until looks exactly the same as the sample doc
  3. Share the doc

Participants/Target Users

I asked 5 participants to help me to this study. They all had experience in Microsoft Word and Google Doc, and they have never used Dropbox paper before.

I defined the target users to be Dropbox Paper first-time-user because I had hypothesised that Dropbox Paper is a relative new product, and it is ideal to attract new users.

User Feedbacks

I took notes while participants “thinking out loud” during the test, and have analysed the data based on errors they made.

Notes organising and analysing

Findings: 3 Key Issues

Here is the errors and user feedbacks based on type.
3 key issues that participants reported especially hard to finish are:

  1. Uploading and placing the graphics.
  2. Embedding music and videos
  3. Typing emojis

Data analysis

Embedding media

Adding music and video file was reported as confusing. Especially when there is a “embed code” choice in the toolbar, users were tempted to embed the Spotify music piece in form of coding. Copying and pasting the link of media is not the first thing come to users’ minds when they were trying to finish this task.

One user also complained that deleting link doesn’t make the embedded music vanishing is weird.

“How can I delete the link since link is the only thing I did to import the music?!”

Graphics placing

Placing the pictures is not extremely hard, but moving them around by drag is not the most easy way for users. All 5 of my participants tried to move their cursors to the corners of the pictures, trying to enlarge or make the pictures smaller.

Emoji

Emoji is the most difficult task in this test. None of the users figured out how to type emojis out. 4 out of 5 tried Google it, and there were 2 of them did not find answer on Google and gave it up.

“If I am at home, I would have quitted trying to find emojis by now.”
The correct way is to type “:”, but clearly nobody would have tried that.

Design Suggestion

Embedding media

All participants easily found and used the toolbar to create list. Adding embedding media icon in the toolbar is easier for users to figure out copying and pasting link. Following screenshot is an example.
