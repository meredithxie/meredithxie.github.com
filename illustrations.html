Strawberry shortcake

Inspired by Kendyll Hillegas

I have always thought strawberry shortcakes are really fun to draw. This painting is inspired by Kendyll Hillegas, and I had a lot of fun painting it.

This painting is done using watercolor pencils, with basic pencil outlining techniques. I believe this cupcake is originally the scran line's work.



Lemon mint cupcake

Inspired by Kendyll Hillegas

Again, this painting is inspired by Kendyll Hillegas. I am personally actually not a big fan of lemon flavored cupcake (red velvet rules), but I like the color combination.

This painting is also done using watercolor pencils. I think this cupcake, again, is originally the Scran Line's work. Scan Line has a lot of fun and amazing pastry illustrations.



Grilled cheese sandwich

Golden crispy and warm

Kendyll Hillegas presented a slow motion drawing grilled cheese sandwish on her personal channel. I recreated the illustration using water color pencil and photoshop to enhance the work.

I loved the color combination and how the water color spreading out on the paper while I was dissolving the color.



Blueberry muffin

A plain and normal blueberry muffin that you take to your field trip

To be honest, I am not a big fan of blueberry muffin, and I did not enjoy painting it out as much as I did with the cupcakes.

However, I still like the idea of painting really mundane food that you see often in real life, so I did spend my time and trying to make a blueberry muffin come to life.



Strawberry lollipop cupcake

A combination of flavor and color

I am a big fan of lollipop. Lollipop was my childhood favorite candy. I love how it looks and how it takes me a long time to finish it.

However, single lollipop illustration simply does not look fun enough on paper, so I combined it with cupcake and strawberries.



Strawberry cream shortcake

Japanese classic strawberry and cream shortcake

I watch a lot of Japanese anime, and some characters are really into strawberry cream shortcake. It became one of my favorite dessert too.

I still like how the dessert looks like in anime instead of on paper with water color. However it is still fun to draw.
