This project is a freelance project I did for a cafe in Beijing named Tien Mee, which mainly sells coffee and different pastries. I am the designer who redesigned their brand website. Due to translation issues, there are some mockups I share here containing placement text. This project took me about seven weeks.

The owner of Tien Mee cafe insisted to use the existing contents and sitemap, so my job was to main improve the visual of the website.

The visual elements

The owner of this cafe is very proud of the ambience the cafe provided. So one of the most important design element is to market such ambience. Here are some example of visual elements I designed and iterated based on the owner's feedback.

I also created a lot of blocks of items the cafe sells, focusing on the popular pastries. These blocks are shown in both hovered and non-hovered status down below.

Since this process involved with some animation, me and the engineer worked closely together, exploring on different options we have to achieve the optimized result. I decided to go with CSS animation instead of Javascripts in the end, because CSS animation loads a lot faster on the server.

Landing page

As the project progress, me and the owner sat closely together, mainly trying to figure out the content on the website. The owner has a lot of stories behind each design decision we made together, so I explored many different ways to design the landing page, and received feedback from the owner. The iteration process took about five weeks.

The other content the owner wanted to emphasize on was the upcoming events. So I researched on many different website and competitors, and learned how others achieve the goal. I ideated on the subject, and here is the result that me and the owner both agreed on.

The blog post is another major milestone for me as a designer. I explored many different font, color and weight, to bring the optimized reading experience.

Lastly, since the website is still under development, there is a chance there will be more design work involved. The next step is to continue helping explore different design options based on needs and helping the development.
